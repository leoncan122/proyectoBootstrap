Do an OLLIE.

For now the only trick that you need to learn is the Ollie.

it's a basic thing, and the first, that you should learn. So the first rule is always to start and land(finish) a ollie or any other trick, it's BEND YOUR KNEES. this is the first and the last step.

But what's happen in the middle of the transition?...

Put your backfoot on the tail of the beast, and the front foot just a little before the screws.
Whit your knees bended (not your body, keep your head in her position, and im saying this because i see a lot of people trying to kiss the floor when they prepare to do a trick) you should jump hitting the skateboard whit your backfoot, jump straight(in the same point), and quickly late do a little kick whit the front foot, and flicking your toe and draggin your table forward.

How can i drag my table straight forward?...

Then you jump you must bend again your backfoot in the air, and scrap your table whit your front foot flicked.

This is all about timing and practice, practice, practice and practice.

Fundamentals

The first thing if you need to know is that you will hurt, there is no way to don't hurt it, is part of the learning and practice, so if you have fear, do it whit fear but do it.

Then when you are ready to ride the beast, just put your foot that going to be placed in front of your, and then put on the other foot that rest on the floor, that will posicionated on the back side of the table, your feet should be over the screws.
Prove to bend your knees a little or all that you feel comfortable, think that more bended more flexibility and near to the floor, so less injuries and danger.

When you feel comfortable to push, you must put the backfoot out of the table bend your knees again, push forward to the point where the leg is straight, and repeat, and repeat, then you can put your backfoot again on the table.
At this point, on movement, going forward, you body should rotates, so you vision will keep seeing the way, but your body is in the same position that your skateboard(table).
